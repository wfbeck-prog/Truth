The Training Wheels Mom: When to Let Go Without Letting Down

A new parenting model for the stage where they still need you, but not in the same way — and pretending otherwise only makes everyone crazy.

By Deborah CruzUpdated May 20258 min read

Share: Facebook · Pinterest · Email ·

There comes a moment when motherhood stops being about holding their hand and starts being about becoming the steady place they can launch from.

That is the part nobody warns you about. The baby years are loud, exhausting, and physically relentless, but at least the job description is obvious. Feed them. Keep them alive. Teach them not to lick grocery cart handles.

But parenting teens, college kids, and adult-ish children is different. They need room. They need trust. They need consequences. They need you close enough to find, but far enough away that they can hear their own voice.

Training wheels are support, not control

Training Wheels Parenting is not helicopter parenting in better shoes. It is the practice of staying present while slowly removing the parts of your protection that are keeping them from learning balance.

You are still there. You are still the base. You are still the person who knows when the silence means peace and when it means something is wrong. But you are no longer supposed to ride the bike for them.

“Letting go is not abandonment. Sometimes it is the most disciplined form of love.”
