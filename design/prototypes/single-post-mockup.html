I Spent 50 Years Shrinking To Fit a World That Was Never Made for Me—I’m Done

A story of lies, deception, trauma — and the radical, messy, life-changing work of reparenting yourself back to who you always were.

By Deborah Cruz Published May 2025 Updated May 2025 8 min read
Share this story Facebook Pinterest Email

There comes a moment in midlife when the story you have been telling yourself starts to sound less like truth and more like survival.

For years, I thought becoming smaller was what made me easier to love. Quieter. More agreeable. Less complicated. Less needy. Less angry. Less myself.

Motherhood made that both harder and easier. Harder because there is no louder mirror than raising children. Easier because there is always someone else to put first, always another need to outrank your own.

The body remembers what the mind tries to bury

Midlife has a way of pulling everything forward. The old wounds. The family stories. The promises we made to survive. The parts of ourselves we hid because they were inconvenient to everyone else.

This is why the new site direction matters. The Truth About Motherhood has always been about telling the truth from inside the life we are actually living — not the polished version we were told to perform.

“The truth keeps growing up with us. The stories change, but the honesty stays.”

What this next chapter opens up

The redesign should make space for motherhood, midlife, perimenopause, health, grief, family, marriage, travel, politics, and opinion without making the site feel scattered. The throughline is Deborah’s voice.

Readers should feel like they are entering a warm editorial home: credible, personal, visual, and easy to browse.

Why the single post page matters

Most traffic will not enter through the homepage. Readers will arrive from Google, Pinterest, Facebook, newsletters, and AI summaries directly into an article. That page has to explain the brand, build trust, invite the next click, and keep the archive alive.

That means every post template needs strong typography, visible author authority, helpful internal links, related posts, a newsletter CTA, and clean SEO-friendly HTML.
