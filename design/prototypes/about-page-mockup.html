The Woman

Debi, in her own words.

The final copy belongs to Debi. This section is built to hold her voice: Chicago roots, bicultural family, language, writing, humor, and the truth behind The TRUTH.

Hi! I’m Deborah Cruz but my friends call me, Debi. I tell the TRUTH about Motherhood and now, I’ve even got the skinny on the teens.

I’m a freckled Mexican from Chicago; first generation Mexican American, the oldest daughter of six children to a Mexican father and a southern mother. I’m the bicultural Marcia Brady, only my parents are Catholic so we share biological parents.

I spoke differently because of the accents I grew up hearing. My brain rapid-fires words with no filter. Being teased for my pressured speech led me to embrace the written word. It’s how I process everything. This is where my love of words and languages began.

The Truth About Debi

When I write, there is no confusion about what I’m saying. I tell you the truth, all of it — the good, the bad, the ugly and the funny.

My unique language situation at home led me to speak four languages: English, Spanish, French and Italian. I did before becoming a mom. Now, I’m fluent in baby, gibberish, tween and teen speak. I can still enjoy foreign films with no subtitles, but that’s about it.

I spent my childhood traveling to Mexico. This is where my love of travel was born. My dad was adamant that we immerse ourselves in the culture. We did, and I’m grateful he did this for us. I do the same for my girls.

The Family

The Big Guy, Bella, Gabi, Stella, Louis, Mazy — and a whole lot of truth.

By fate, I ended up in the wrong place at the right time — Purdue University — and met the 6’5” man who is everything I never knew I always wanted. He proposed after not quite four months of dating, thanks to a little help from Salma Hayek. Fools Rush In inspired the Big Guy to propose.

We graduated college, got married, waited five years, went to New Orleans and got pregnant on street pizza. Best night of our lives. Then we grew up… sort of. Or maybe we just created our own little fun group of people to hang with.

When I started this blog, Bella and Gabi were little. They have grown up on The TRUTH. Readers who have been here for a while remember the ballerina toddler years, the red Chanel lipstick, the pearls, and the kind of chaos only motherhood can produce. Now we are deep in the teen and launching-pad years, and honestly, I love this stage too.

Today, the Midwest house is full of family, laughter, eye rolls, food, travel plans, and dogs: Stella, our French Mastiff, plus Louis and Mazy, our two French Bulldogs. Long-time readers may remember Lola, our beloved Victorian Bulldog. She is no longer with us, but she will always be part of our family story.

Our favorite thing to do is be together — laughing, trying new foods, experiencing new cultures, and exploring the world. For us, family is everything and everything is better together.

The Adventures

Mexico, Disney, Broadway, food, culture, and seeing the world together.

This section gives travel and Disney their own place in the story instead of burying them inside the bio.

Travel is part of how Deborah understands family, culture, memory, and motherhood.

Childhood trips to Mexico shaped her love of language, food, family, tradition, and cultural immersion. Those experiences became part of how she parents and how she writes.

We are officially Walt Disney World addicts. My first pixie-dusted trip was on our honeymoon, and it convinced me Disney could be as romantic as walking hand in hand in Europe. Disney and Broadway are my jams.

Travel stories on The TRUTH are not just destination recaps. They are about family, culture, identity, food, memory, and the way the world looks different when you see it through your children’s eyes.
